Kabab

Description

Kebab (UK: /kɪˈbæb/, US: /kɪˈbɑːb/; Persian: كباب,[1] kabāb, Arabic: كباب,[2] [kaˈbaːb]; Turkish: kebap, [cebɑp]), kabob (North American), or kebap or kabab is roasted meat that originates from the Middle East but has been popularised by Iranian cuisine & Turkish cuisine. Many variants of the category are popular around the world, including the skewered shish kebab and the doner kebab with bread.

Ingredients

  • meat
  • onions
  • garlic
  • olive oil
  • salt
  • pepper
  • spices
  • bell peppers
  • mushrooms
  • tomatoes

first three Steps

  1. Marinate the meat (and vegetables if using) with yogurt, spices, herbs, and oil for a few hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Skewer the meat (and vegetables if using) on metal or wooden skewers. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes before using to prevent burning.
  3. Grill the kababs over high heat on a charcoal grill, gas grill, oven, or grill pan until cooked through and charred on the outside. Turn them occasionally and brush them with oil or butter if desired.
